In our daily life, people tend to link colors with everyday objects, emotion, sound or smell.

For example, light yellow is mostly related to lemon; dark blue is related to sad feeling; pink is related to sweet smell.

This is explained by Synesthesia.

Synesthesia is a perceptual phenomenon in which stimulation of one sensory or cognitive pathway leads to automatic, involuntary experiences in a second sensory or cognitive pathway.
